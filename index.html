University of Missouri

What started as just an intership into a fantastic job to have in college. I worked as a graphic designer in the marketing depart for the Mizzou bookstore. I created in store signage, brainstormed marketing, campaigns & created content for the Mizzou Bookstore website.

McCord Design Group

My first intership out of school was paid & lasted 6 weeks. I initially interviewed for a permanent position, but because I was so green, instead offered me an intership. It was great expeirence working for a small design firm. I was able to produce some cool things & learn a ton.

The Growth Partnership

My first real job out school. The Growth Partnership is a small marketing firm that services CPAs & financial firms. It wasn't the most exciting job, but it gave me a great foundation that started my career. It was here where I started dabbling in web development. After almost four years though, I was ready for a channge.

EllieMae

My neighbor suggested I apply for a graphic design roll at her company that involed some HTML & CSS. It sounded fantastic and I jumped at the opportunity. It turned out to be not-so-great due to a terrible boss, but I did learn that graphic design was getting stale for me and coding was my true passion. I quit after ten months whithout anything official lined up.

TeeTurtle

TeeTurtle was a company that had been on my radar since graduating from college, and on a fluke checked there website to find they they were in fact hiring for a web dev. I applied. A week later I had an HR phone interview scheduled for Tuesday. Wednesday I had an in person interview and Thursday I had a job offer. Then in the middle of salary negotiations, they asked me if I'd be willing to move to Denver where they were openning a new office. Pretty stoked for this new adventure.
